Foundations · 2010 – 2013

Three places, three things that still run underneath everything

Maruti Suzuki — Delhi

First job. Graduate Engineer Trainee, then Assistant Manager in R&D. The Japanese shop-floor discipline landed early — managers on the floor, egalitarian teams, 5S as a working philosophy: sort, set in order, shine, standardize, sustain. Watched how a large company's functions actually sync. That clarity around operational detail has never left.

Thales — Paris

Strategic Designer at their fledgling Design Centre, secured via cold email. My boss Didier spent hours walking me through design thinking and the real mechanics of changing a 70,000-person company. Shadowed meetings, ran workshops, helped build the space — IDEO and Stanford d.school as the reference points. First real schooling in translating between innovation and scale.

Board of Innovation — Antwerp

Internship. Given a free hand to analyse new venture possibilities for Sappi — the global woodfibre giant looking to diversify using their existing assets. Framed the opportunities, presented to Sappi management. Watched BoI run venture-building for other clients at the same time. A masterclass in opportunity identification before I knew the phrase.

Operational discipline from Japan. Design methods from France. Venture thinking from Belgium. The stack I still run on.

०५ About

I grew up in a small town, went to a Ramakrishna Mission boarding school, and learned early that the world was mine to figure out.

Engineering at Delhi College of Engineering, then strategic design at TU Delft. Delft cracked me open.

I've built innovation studios inside conglomerates and a Web3 startup from a Paris apartment. Raised through crypto winters. Shipped products people actually use. Walked away when the chapter was done.

Lived in Delft, The Hague, Antwerp, Barcelona, Paris. Back in Mumbai now — with Subhasree and our two kids.

I'm drawn to how people live. The next thing I build will probably sit at the edge of culture, capital, and emerging tech.
